01 — Why GraphReFly

Three reasons you need this.

01

Stop Drowning in Information

You're buried under emails, alerts, feeds, messages. GraphReFly is a universal reduction engine: describe what matters, and it watches, filters, prioritizes, and alerts.

02

Trust AI by Understanding It

Every decision is traceable. Ask "why was this flagged?" and get a causal chain from source to conclusion — structural causality that persists across sessions.

03

Describe It, Don't Code It

Tell an LLM what you need in plain English. It composes a graph. You review a simplified flow view. It runs. You tweak by talking, not coding.

02 — Mechanism

How it works.

Step 01

You describe

Tell it what you care about in plain language. What to watch, what matters, what to ignore.

Step 02

LLM composes a graph

An LLM translates your intent into a reactive graph — nodes, edges, operators. You review a simplified flow view.

Step 03

Graph runs reactively

The graph runs persistently. Checkpoints state. Traces every decision. You tweak by talking.

03 — Real Scenarios

What you'll build.

Scenario / Email Triage

Email Triage

"Flag urgent client emails, summarize the rest, archive noise." A reactive graph watches your inbox, classifies by urgency, and delivers a daily brief.

Scenario / Spending Alerts

Spending Alerts

"Watch transactions, flag unusual spending, weekly digest." Nodes monitor feeds, operators detect anomalies, and a sink composes your summary.

Scenario / Knowledge Mgmt

Knowledge Management

"Watch research feeds, extract insights, build a knowledge graph." Sources ingest, LLM nodes distill, and derived nodes weave connections.

Scenario / CI/CD Monitor

CI/CD Monitor

"Alert on failed builds, correlate with recent commits, suggest rollback." Reactive tracing from failure to cause in seconds.

Scenario / Meeting Prep

Meeting Prep

"Before each meeting, pull context from email, Slack, docs." Nodes watch your calendar and compose pre-meeting briefs automatically.

04 — Code

A quick taste.

graphrefly-ts state · derived · effect 
const count   = state(0);
const doubled = derived([count], (c) => c * 2);

effect([doubled], (d) => console.log(d));

count.down([[DATA, 1]]);  // logs: 2

Reactive primitives. No framework. No build step. Just signals, derived computations, and effects.
