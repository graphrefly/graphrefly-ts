The Problem

Three headaches.

01

Volume Without Boundaries

Information multiplies across sources — email, Slack, feeds, APIs, logs. No single system sees it all. You become the integration layer.

GraphReFly

Distributed Reactive Graphs

A single reactive topology connects every source. Distributed graphs federate across services. One graph sees what ten dashboards can't.

Demo: Email Triage →
02

Velocity Without Rhythm

Markets move in milliseconds. Policies update quarterly. Emails arrive in bursts. Each source has its own clock — and your cron job missed the one that mattered.

GraphReFly

Push-Based Propagation

Each node reacts the instant its source changes. No polling intervals. No missed beats. No stale snapshots accumulating in context.

Demo: Spending Alerts →
03

Action Without Memory

You vibe-coded a fix. It worked. Next month, nobody knows why — or whether it's still correct. Disposable solutions you can't repeat, explain, or build upon.

GraphReFly

Persistent Causal Chain

Every decision traces back through a structural causal chain. Persistent, inspectable, composable. Change upstream — downstream knows it may no longer hold.

The Vision

Four requirements.

For human + LLM co-operation to work at scale, the system must satisfy all four. Miss one, the whole thing breaks.

Human / LLM Symmetry
Either side composes, reviews, modifies
Human Audit
Every decision traceable
LLM-Safe Creation
Constrained error space
Security
Per-node access control. Multi-tenant isolation.
Click a block to explore
Each requirement builds on the ones below. Security is the foundation — without it, nothing is trustworthy. LLM-safe creation and human audit sit in the middle — both require secure infrastructure. At the apex, human/LLM symmetry requires all three: safe creation, auditability, and enforced boundaries.
